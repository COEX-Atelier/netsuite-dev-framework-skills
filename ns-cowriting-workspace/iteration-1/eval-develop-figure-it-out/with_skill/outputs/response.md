### 2.1 Strategic Context

The organization is a mid-market manufacturing company operating at the complexity and scale characteristic of a Tier 2 NetSuite implementation. Current systems are insufficient to support the operational demands of a growing manufacturing environment, creating pressure to consolidate on a unified ERP platform that provides real-time visibility, automated workflows, and integrated financial controls.

### 2.2 Primary Business Pain Points

**Inventory Accuracy**
The company is experiencing persistent inventory discrepancy between physical stock and system records. In a manufacturing environment, inaccurate inventory data cascades into production planning failures, missed customer commitments, and inflated safety stock buffers that tie up working capital. The inability to trust on-hand quantities forces manual cycle counting and exception management that consume significant operations staff time.

**Manual Accounts Payable Process**
The current AP process is largely manual — invoice receipt, matching, approval routing, and payment execution are performed outside an integrated system. This creates risk of duplicate payments, missed early-payment discounts, strained vendor relationships due to payment delays, and an AP close cycle that extends the monthly financial close. In a manufacturing context, where supplier relationships and material availability are operationally critical, AP inefficiency directly threatens production continuity.

### 2.3 Business Drivers for NetSuite Implementation

| Driver | Business Impact |
|--------|----------------|
| Inventory accuracy and real-time visibility | Reduce production disruption, lower safety stock cost, eliminate manual reconciliation |
| Automated AP and procurement workflow | Accelerate invoice-to-payment cycle, reduce duplicate payments, enable early-pay discount capture |
| Integrated financial and operational data | Single source of truth across manufacturing, warehouse, and finance functions |
| Scalability for mid-market growth | Support increased transaction volume, additional product lines, and potential multi-location expansion |
| Audit-readiness and financial controls | Strengthen internal controls to meet audit requirements and reduce close cycle time |

### 2.4 Expected Business Outcomes

A successful NetSuite implementation is expected to deliver:

- Inventory record accuracy at or above 98%, enabling reliable MRP and production scheduling
- AP processing time reduced by an estimated 50–70% through electronic invoice capture and automated three-way matching
- Monthly financial close cycle shortened through elimination of manual reconciliation steps
- Improved vendor relationships and access to early-payment discounts through consistent, on-time payment execution
- Management visibility through real-time dashboards spanning operations and finance
