To start your migration from QuickBooks to NetSuite, we will follow the **Phase 1: Discovery** workflow. Given that you have three subsidiaries, 50 users, and require a full historical data migration for O2C and P2P processes, this project is classified as a **Tier 1 Brownfield Implementation**.

This classification ensures we have the necessary governance and documentation to manage the complexity of a multi-subsidiary rollout and a legacy system replacement.

### How We Start: Phase 1 - Discovery

Our immediate goal is to understand your business requirements in detail to create a solid Project Charter and Business Requirements Document (BRD). Here is the step-by-step approach:

#### 1. Stakeholder Interviews
We need to meet with key representatives from Finance, Sales, Operations, and IT.
- **Finance:** Focus on the Chart of Accounts (COA) for the 3 subsidiaries, consolidation requirements, and historical data needs.
- **Sales/Ops:** Map the Order-to-Cash (O2C) and Procure-to-Pay (P2P) flows.
- **IT:** Analyze the QuickBooks data structure and any external tools (e.g., Bill.com, Expensify) currently in use.

#### 2. "As-Is" Process Review
Since this is a Brownfield project, we must document your current processes in QuickBooks. We will identify:
- Manual workarounds or Excel-based steps that should be automated in NetSuite.
- Current pain points in O2C and P2P.
- Data cleansing requirements for historical records.

#### 3. System Landscape Analysis
We will map out every system that interacts with your financials. We'll decide which will be replaced by NetSuite, which will be integrated, and which will be decommissioned.

#### 4. Scope Definition & BRD
We will produce a clear list of what is In-Scope for the initial Go-Live and what might be deferred to a second phase. This will be formalized in the **Business Requirements Document (BRD)**, which will serve as the source of truth for the implementation.
